The Kinetic Theory of Gases Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of the kinetic theory of gases, including the ideal gas model, molecular motion, pressure, temperature, Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, degrees of freedom, and specific heats. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering the kinetic theory, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the ideal gas law from the kinetic theory $P V = \frac{1}{3} N m v_{\text{rms}}^2$.

  2. Derive the relation between temperature and kinetic energy $\frac{1}{2} m v_{\text{rms}}^2 = \frac{3}{2} k T$.

  3. Derive the Maxwell-Boltzmann speed distribution $f(v)$.

  4. Derive the most probable speed $v_{\text{mp}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 k T}{m}}$.

  5. Derive the average speed $v_{\text{avg}} = \sqrt{\frac{8 k T}{\pi m}}$.

  6. Derive the RMS speed $v_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\frac{3 k T}{m}}$.

  7. Derive the internal energy for an ideal gas $U = \frac{f}{2} n R T$.

  8. Derive the mean free path $\lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} \pi d^2 n}$.

  9. Derive the relation $C_P - C_V = R$ (Mayer’s relation).

  10. Derive the ratio of specific heats $\gamma = 1 + \frac{2}{f}$.

  11. Derive Avogadro’s hypothesis using the ideal gas law.

  12. Derive the total kinetic energy of a gas $KE_{\text{total}} = \frac{3}{2} n R T$.

  13. Derive the number density formula $n = \frac{N}{V}$.

  14. Derive the energy distribution from the Maxwell-Boltzmann speed distribution.

  15. Derive the equipartition theorem for energy per degree of freedom.
