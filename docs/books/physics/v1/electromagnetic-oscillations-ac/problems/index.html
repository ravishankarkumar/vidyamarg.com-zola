Electromagnetic Oscillations and Alternating Current Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of electromagnetic oscillations and alternating current (AC) circuits, including calculations of oscillation frequency, impedance, resonance, and power in AC circuits. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering electromagnetism, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

  3. Derive the total energy in an LC circuit $U = \frac{Q_0^2}{2C}$.

  4. Derive the impedance of a series RLC circuit $Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2}$.

  5. Derive the resonance frequency of an RLC circuit $\omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{L C}}$.

  6. Derive the average power in an AC circuit $P_{\text{avg}} = \frac{V_0 I_0}{2} \cos \phi$.

  7. Derive the power factor $\cos \phi$ in an RL circuit $\cos \phi = \frac{R}{Z}$.

  8. Derive the phase angle in an RLC circuit $\tan \phi = \frac{X_L - X_C}{R}$.

  9. Derive the inductive reactance $X_L = \omega L$.

  10. Derive the capacitive reactance $X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C}$.

  11. Derive the rms voltage $V_{\text{rms}} = \frac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}}$ in an AC circuit.

  12. Derive the current oscillation in an LC circuit $I = -\omega Q_0 \sin (\omega t + \phi)$.

  13. Derive the voltage across a capacitor in an AC circuit $V_C = I X_C$.

  14. Derive the condition for maximum current in an RLC circuit at resonance.

  15. Derive the energy oscillation between $L$ and $C$ in an LC circuit.
