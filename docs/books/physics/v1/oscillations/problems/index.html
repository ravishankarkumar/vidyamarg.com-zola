Oscillations Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of oscillatory motion, including simple harmonic motion (SHM), energy in oscillatory systems, damped and forced oscillations, and resonance. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering oscillations, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

  1. Derive the equation of motion for SHM $a = -\omega^2 x$.

  2. Derive the period of a spring-mass system $T = 2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$.

  3. Derive the total energy in SHM $E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$.

  4. Derive the velocity in SHM as a function of position $v = \omega \sqrt{A^2 - x^2}$.

  5. Derive the period of a simple pendulum $T = 2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$ for small angles.

  6. Derive the solution to damped SHM $x(t) = A e^{-\gamma t} \cos(\omega' t + \phi)$.

  7. Derive the maximum amplitude at resonance for a forced oscillator.

  8. Derive the Q-factor $Q = \frac{\omega_0}{2 \gamma}$ for a damped oscillator.

  9. Derive the velocity and acceleration in SHM from $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)$.

  10. Derive the energy oscillation in SHM showing $K + U = \text{constant}$.

  11. Derive the damped frequency $\omega' = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \gamma^2}$.

  12. Derive the maximum kinetic energy of a pendulum using small-angle approximation.

  13. Derive the energy decay in a damped oscillator $E(t) \propto e^{-2 \gamma t}$.

  14. Derive the resonance frequency for a forced oscillator.

  15. Derive the maximum acceleration in SHM $a_{\text{max}} = A \omega^2$.
