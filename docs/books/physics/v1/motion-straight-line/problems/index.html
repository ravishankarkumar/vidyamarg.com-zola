Motion Along a Straight Line Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of motion along a straight line, including displacement, velocity, acceleration, kinematic equations, and free fall. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the kinematic equation $v = u + at$ using the definition of acceleration.

  2. Derive the kinematic equation $x = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2$ by integrating velocity.

  3. Derive the kinematic equation $v^2 = u^2 + 2ax$ using the other two equations.

  4. Derive the average velocity formula $v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{1}{2}(u + v)$ for constant acceleration.

  5. Derive the displacement of a particle in free fall starting from rest.

  6. Derive the dimension of velocity using base SI units.

  7. Derive the time to reach maximum height for an object thrown upward under gravity.

  8. Derive the dimension of acceleration using base SI units.

  9. Derive the maximum height reached by an object thrown upward under gravity.

  10. Derive the displacement of a particle with constant acceleration using $v = u + at$ and $v_{\text{avg}}$.

  11. Derive the velocity-time relationship for an object in free fall starting from rest.

  12. Derive the position-time relationship for a particle with constant velocity.

  13. Derive the displacement of a particle given $v(t) = 2t + 3$ from $t = 0$ to $t = 2$.

  14. Derive the acceleration of a particle given $x(t) = 3t^2 - 4t + 1$ at $t = 1$.

  15. Derive the total displacement of a particle that accelerates from rest at $2 , m/s^2$ for 3 s, then decelerates at $-1 , m/s^2$ for 2 s.
