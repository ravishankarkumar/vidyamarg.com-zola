Circuits Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of DC circuits, including Kirchhoff's laws, series and parallel resistor combinations, capacitors in circuits, RC circuits, and their applications. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering circuit analysis, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive Kirchhoff's first law based on the conservation of charge.

  2. Derive Kirchhoff's second law based on the conservation of energy.

  3. Derive the equivalent resistance for resistors in series $R_{\text{eq}} = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_n$.

  4. Derive the equivalent resistance for resistors in parallel $\frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{R_n}$.

  5. Derive the equivalent capacitance for capacitors in series $\frac{1}{C_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{C_n}$.

  6. Derive the equivalent capacitance for capacitors in parallel $C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2 + \cdots + C_n$.

  7. Derive the charging equation for an RC circuit $Q = C \mathcal{E} \left( 1 - e^{-t / RC} \right)$.

  8. Derive the discharging equation for an RC circuit $Q = Q_0 e^{-t / RC}$.

  9. Derive the terminal voltage of a battery with internal resistance $V = \mathcal{E} - I r$.

  10. Derive the current in an RC circuit during charging $I = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R} e^{-t / RC}$.

  11. Derive the voltage across the capacitor in a charging RC circuit $V_C = \mathcal{E} \left( 1 - e^{-t / RC} \right)$.

  12. Derive the time constant $\tau = RC$ and its significance in RC circuits.

  13. Derive the voltage across the resistor in a discharging RC circuit $V_R = -\frac{Q_0}{C} e^{-t / RC}$.

  14. Derive the current distribution in parallel resistors $I_i = \frac{V}{R_i}$.

  15. Derive the charge distribution in series capacitors $Q$ is the same on each capacitor.
