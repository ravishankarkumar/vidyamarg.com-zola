Entropy and the Second Law of Thermodynamics Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of entropy, the second law of thermodynamics, heat engines, refrigerators, and the Carnot cycle. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering thermodynamics, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the equivalence of the Kelvin-Planck and Clausius statements of the second law.

  2. Derive the entropy change for a reversible isothermal process $\Delta S = \frac{Q_{\text{rev}}}{T}$.

  3. Derive the entropy change for a constant volume process $\Delta S = n C_V \ln \left( \frac{T_2}{T_1} \right)$.

  4. Derive the entropy change in an irreversible free expansion.

  5. Derive the efficiency of a heat engine $e = 1 - \frac{Q_C}{Q_H}$.

  6. Derive the coefficient of performance for a refrigerator $COP = \frac{Q_C}{W}$.

  7. Derive the Carnot efficiency $e_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$.

  8. Derive the Carnot refrigerator COP $COP_{\text{Carnot}} = \frac{T_C}{T_H - T_C}$.

  9. Derive the work done in the Carnot cycle $W = n R (T_H - T_C) \ln \left( \frac{V_2}{V_1} \right)$.

  10. Derive the entropy change for heat transfer between two reservoirs.

  11. Derive the second law in terms of entropy $\Delta S_{\text{total}} \geq 0$.

  12. Derive the relation $\frac{Q_H}{T_H} = \frac{Q_C}{T_C}$ for a Carnot cycle.

  13. Derive the irreversibility of free expansion using entropy.

  14. Derive the efficiency limitation of a real engine compared to a Carnot engine.

  15. Derive the entropy change for an adiabatic reversible process (isentropic process).
