Electromagnetic Waves Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of electromagnetic waves, including calculations of wave speed, wavelength, frequency, intensity, and energy density, as well as properties like polarization and the electromagnetic spectrum. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering electromagnetism, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the wave equation for an electromagnetic wave $\nabla^2 \vec{E} = \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \vec{E}}{\partial t^2}$.

  2. Derive the relationship between $E_0$ and $B_0$ in an electromagnetic wave $E_0 = c B_0$.

  3. Derive the average intensity of an electromagnetic wave $I = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 c E_0^2$.

  4. Derive the energy density of an electromagnetic wave $u = \epsilon_0 E^2$.

  5. Derive the Poynting vector for a plane electromagnetic wave $\vec{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \vec{E} \times \vec{B}$.

  6. Derive the wave number $k$ for a wave with wavelength $\lambda$, $k = \frac{2 \pi}{\lambda}$.

  7. Derive the angular frequency $\omega$ for a wave with frequency $f$, $\omega = 2 \pi f$.

  8. Derive the speed of an electromagnetic wave in vacuum $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$.

  9. Derive the relationship between frequency $f$ and wavelength $\lambda$, $f \lambda = c$.

  10. Derive the average energy density using $B_0$, $u_{\text{avg}} = \frac{1}{2} \frac{B_{\text{rms}}^2}{\mu_0}$.

  11. Derive the electric field amplitude $E_0$ from intensity $I$, $E_0 = \sqrt{\frac{2 I}{\epsilon_0 c}}$.

  12. Derive the magnetic field amplitude $B_0$ from intensity $I$, $B_0 = \sqrt{\frac{2 I \mu_0}{c}}$.

  13. Derive the wavelength of a visible light wave with a given frequency.

  14. Derive the frequency of an X-ray with a given wavelength.

  15. Derive the polarization direction of a plane electromagnetic wave.
