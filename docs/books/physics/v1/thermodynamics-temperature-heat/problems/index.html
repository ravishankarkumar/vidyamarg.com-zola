Temperature, Heat, and the First Law of Thermodynamics Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of thermodynamics, including temperature scales, thermal equilibrium, heat transfer mechanisms, specific heat, calorimetry, thermal expansion, and the first law of thermodynamics. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering thermodynamics, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the Kelvin-Celsius conversion $T (\text{K}) = T (\text{°C}) + 273.15$.

  2. Derive the Celsius-Fahrenheit conversion $T (\text{°F}) = \frac{9}{5} T (\text{°C}) + 32$.

  3. Derive the conduction equation $Q = \frac{k A \Delta T t}{L}$.

  4. Derive the Stefan-Boltzmann law for net power $P_{\text{net}} = \sigma A e (T^4 - T_0^4)$.

  5. Derive the heat equation $Q = m c \Delta T$.

  6. Derive the calorimetry equation for mixing two substances.

  7. Derive the linear expansion formula $\Delta L = L_0 \alpha \Delta T$.

  8. Derive the first law of thermodynamics $\Delta U = Q - W$.

  9. Derive the work done in an isobaric process $W = P \Delta V$.

  10. Derive the work done in an isothermal process for an ideal gas $W = n R T \ln\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)$.

  11. Derive the adiabatic relation $P V^\gamma = \text{constant}$.

  12. Derive the thermal resistance formula $R = \frac{L}{k A}$.

  13. Derive the internal energy change for an ideal gas $\Delta U = n C_V \Delta T$.

  14. Derive the volume expansion formula $\Delta V = V_0 \beta \Delta T$.

  15. Derive the ideal gas thermometer relation $T \propto P$.
