Relativity Problems

This section provides 100 problems to test your understanding of special relativity, including calculations of the Lorentz factor, time dilation, length contraction, relativistic velocity, momentum, kinetic energy, and mass-energy conversion, as well as applications like nuclear reactions and spacecraft navigation. Inspired by JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET exam patterns, these problems are tailored for exam preparation, offering a mix of numerical, conceptual, and derivation-based challenges. NEET-style problems (66–100) are formatted as multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to match the exam’s objective format. Problems are organized by type to support progressive learning and build confidence in mastering modern physics, a key topic for JEE/NEET success.

Derivation Problems

  1. Derive the Lorentz factor $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.

  2. Derive the time dilation formula $\Delta t = \gamma \Delta t_0$.

  3. Derive the length contraction formula $L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$.

  4. Derive the relativistic velocity addition formula $u = \frac{u' + v}{1 + \frac{u' v}{c^2}}$.

  5. Derive the relativistic momentum $p = \gamma m_0 v$.

  6. Derive the relativistic kinetic energy $KE = (\gamma - 1) m_0 c^2$.

  7. Derive the mass-energy equivalence $E = mc^2$.

  8. Derive the energy-momentum relation $E^2 = (pc)^2 + (m_0 c^2)^2$.

  9. Derive the time dilation for a moving muon’s lifetime.

  10. Derive the relativistic momentum of a particle moving at $v = 0.9c$.

  11. Derive the kinetic energy of a particle at low speeds ($v \ll c$).

  12. Derive the total energy of a photon using $E = pc$.

  13. Derive the length contraction for a moving rod at $v = 0.8c$.

  14. Derive the velocity addition for a projectile fired from a moving rocket.

  15. Derive the rest energy of an electron in eV.
